What "DCS" means and why it matters

DCS = Dealing in Virtual Currencies. Added to PCMLTFA in June 2020, this MSB activity category covers any business that:

  • Exchanges crypto for fiat or other crypto (spot trading, OTC, brokerage)
  • Transfers crypto on behalf of clients (wallet operators, payment processors)
  • Holds crypto in custody for clients (custodians, exchange wallets)

If you do any of these activities for the public — domestic Canadian or foreign-served Canadian customers — MSB registration with DCS activity flagged is mandatory under PCMLTFA. There is no de minimis threshold; even small operators must register.

FINTRAC application — what's different for crypto

The FINTRAC pre-registration form is the same for crypto MSBs as standard MSBs, with one critical difference: tick the DCS activity box. This flags your registration as crypto-engaged and triggers:

  • Listing on the public FINTRAC MSB registry with DCS activity disclosed
  • Crypto-specific examination focus during FINTRAC reviews
  • LVCTR + Travel Rule reporting workflow setup
  • Higher banking scrutiny (Canadian banks apply enhanced DD on DCS-registered MSBs)

Timeline — typical 4–8 weeks

  1. Strategy + AML program build (weeks 1–3): crypto-specific risk assessment, transaction monitoring rules, Travel Rule procedures.
  2. Compliance officer appointment + training (weeks 2–4): CAMS or ICA training, FINTRAC examination preparation.
  3. Pre-registration submission (week 3): online FINTRAC portal with DCS flagged.
  4. FINTRAC review (weeks 3–7): 30 days from complete submission. MSB number issued.
  5. Banking onboarding (weeks 4–8, parallel): RBC, BMO, TD MSB onboarding teams.

Penalties for unregistered crypto operations in Canada

  • Administrative monetary penalties up to $500,000 CAD per violation under PCMLTFA
  • Criminal prosecution: fines up to $2M CAD and/or 5 years imprisonment
  • Public listing on FINTRAC enforcement page — significant reputational damage
  • Banking shutdown: Canadian banks routinely close accounts of unregistered MSBs once detected
  • Customer asset freezes potential during enforcement

Operating an unregistered crypto exchange in Canada is the highest-risk regulatory mistake in the FINTRAC enforcement universe. Don't.

FAQ — Canada crypto MSB

Is MSB registration mandatory for Canadian crypto exchanges?

Yes. Since June 1, 2020, persons and entities dealing in virtual currencies (DCS) are required to register with FINTRAC as MSBs. Operating a crypto exchange in Canada without MSB registration is illegal under PCMLTFA and triggers administrative monetary penalties up to $500,000 CAD per violation, plus potential criminal prosecution carrying fines up to $2M and/or 5 years imprisonment.

What is DCS in Canadian MSB context?

DCS = Dealing in Virtual Currencies — the MSB activity category added in June 2020 covering crypto exchange, transfer, and custody for clients. When applying for FINTRAC MSB registration, crypto operators must explicitly flag DCS activity. This triggers crypto-specific obligations: LVCTR reporting (transactions ≥$10,000 CAD within 5 business days), Travel Rule compliance (transfers ≥$1,000 CAD), and source-of-funds verification using blockchain analytics.

What additional costs apply to a crypto MSB vs standard MSB?

Crypto MSB (DCS) costs $12K–$30K vs $8K–$25K for standard MSB. Incremental costs: blockchain analytics tools (Chainalysis, Elliptic, TRM Labs $5K+/year), Travel Rule compliance tooling (Notabene, Sumsub Travel Rule, VerifyVASP $1K–$10K/year), enhanced KYC for source-of-funds on crypto deposits, and crypto-specific risk assessment depth in the AML program.

Can a foreign crypto exchange register as Canada FMSB?

Yes. The Foreign MSB (FMSB) pathway lets non-Canadian businesses serve Canadian crypto customers without Canadian incorporation. Same FINTRAC framework, same DCS obligations, but no Canadian entity required (Canadian agent mandatory). Mandatory since June 2020 for any foreign business serving Canadian crypto customers.

How does Travel Rule work for Canadian crypto MSBs?

For crypto transfers ≥$1,000 CAD, the originating MSB must transmit originator and beneficiary information to the receiving VASP. This typically uses tools like Notabene, Sumsub Travel Rule, or VerifyVASP ($1K–$10K/year). The receiving VASP must verify the information matches their KYC records before crediting. Failure to comply triggers FINTRAC enforcement.
