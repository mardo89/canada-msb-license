Why FINTRAC charges $0 (and why the total still costs money)

FINTRAC is unique among major financial regulators globally — it charges nothing for MSB registration, registration renewal, or annual supervision. No application fee. No annual fee. No surety bond. No minimum capital.

Compare to:

  • Lithuania CASP: €5,000–€10,000 Bank of Lithuania fee + €125,000 capital
  • US state MTLs: $1,500–$25,000 per state × 49 states + surety bonds $10K–$7M per state
  • Singapore PSA: S$10,000 MAS fee + S$250K–$5M capital
  • Dubai VARA: AED 100,000–500,000 fee + AED 1.5M–7M capital

So why does Canada MSB still cost $8K–$25K? Because FINTRAC requires a comprehensive AML compliance program before approving registration, and that program needs documented policies, a trained compliance officer, transaction monitoring, and reporting workflows. Building all of that from scratch costs $5K–$15K minimum. The cost is the work, not the regulator.

Hidden costs founders forget

  1. Banking onboarding cycles ($1,000–$5,000). Canadian banks (RBC, BMO, TD) have dedicated MSB onboarding teams but still ask for 30–100 page DD questionnaires. Compliance officer time + legal review $1K–$5K per bank attempt. Plan for 2 banks.
  2. Provincial securities registration if you touch securities ($5K–$50K+). If your activity touches token issuance, security tokens, or certain derivatives, you may also need OSC (Ontario), AMF (Quebec), or BCSC (BC) registration. Separate regime — adds significantly.
  3. Effectiveness review every 2 years ($3,000–$10,000). FINTRAC-required independent review of your AML program. Many MSBs forget until FINTRAC examination triggers it.
  4. FINTRAC examination preparation ($5,000–$15,000). FINTRAC examines MSBs every 2-4 years on average. Examination prep + response is unbudgeted compliance officer time.
  5. Sanctions screening tooling ($2,000–$10,000/year). Free options exist (UN sanctions list direct query) but commercial tools (LexisNexis, World-Check, Refinitiv) are standard for institutional-credible MSBs.
  6. Crypto-friendly EMI alternatives ($1,000–$5,000 onboarding). If Canadian banks reject DCS-registered MSBs, fintech alternatives (Wealthsimple Cash, Mogo, ATB) may be needed.

FAQ — Canada MSB cost breakdown

What's the absolute minimum to set up a Canada MSB?

~$8,000 for a non-crypto MSB (FX dealer, money transmitter) with founder-as-compliance-officer. Anything less means cutting corners on AML program (which fails FINTRAC review) or skipping legal review (regulatory risk). $8K is the honest floor for non-crypto.

Why does crypto MSB cost more than standard MSB?

Three add-ons specific to crypto (DCS): blockchain analytics ($3K+/year for Chainalysis or TRM Labs), Travel Rule tooling ($1K-$10K/year for Notabene/VerifyVASP), and crypto-specific AML program depth (additional $4K–$8K vs standard MSB program). Total crypto premium: ~$8K–$15K above standard MSB.

Does FINTRAC really charge $0?

Yes — $0. No application fee, no annual fee, no renewal fee. FINTRAC's MSB framework is unique among major regulators globally for this. Renewal every 2 years is also free. The only government cost is your Canadian incorporation if going domestic MSB ($1.5K–$3K one-time).

Can I get a Canada MSB for under $5,000?

Realistically no. Anyone quoting under $5K is selling you a generic AML template that will fail FINTRAC review or omitting compliance officer training entirely. The $8K minimum reflects what genuinely-operational setups cost when FINTRAC actually approves the registration.

Is Canada MSB still cheaper than Lithuania CASP for EU customers?

For NA customers yes; for EU customers no. Canada MSB doesn't grant EU passport — to legally serve EU retail you also need Lithuania CASP (~$50K–$120K). For NA-focused operators, Canada MSB alone is dramatically cheaper than Lithuania alone. For EU-focused, Lithuania CASP is cheaper than Canada MSB + ineligible-to-serve-EU.
